The story of how I learned to code myself out of a job!

(or the story of why I applied to LC101!)

My story begins in 2010, when I was hired by U.S. Bank. I was one of the first people hired to begin work in a brand new department. Within a years' time the department had grown from 20 employees to 150. By year two it had grown to 400 employees. As the department grew so did its reporting needs and I was given the job of building these reports.

It just so happened I had no idea what I was doing but I was excited to meet the challenge. So I took courses to expand my knowledge of microsoft excel and Access databases. I used this knowledge to build databases for every purpose from reporting to workflow to payroll. For a long time these processes were enough, but that time, like all things, came to an end.

In late 2015 our humble reporting team was swallowed up by the company wide reporting team. Where we were laughed at for building reports in Access databases like cavemen. After a healthy amount of ridicule we were told there was a better way, and all we had to do was accept structured query language as our personal lord and savior. So from that day I was baptised into programming and given the title of business analyst.

In the interest of full disclosure, I was a high school dropout and it had been over a decade since I had done that, so to say I was nervous about my job depending on my ability to learn this new skill is an understatement. And yet I took to it rather well, I really surprised myself. In fact before too long I had learned how to (via the miracle of code) automate nearly every process, I was responsible for. It was at this point I was laid off.

THE END
